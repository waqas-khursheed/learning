# Common Idioms Aur Expressions (Native Speakers Jo Use Karte Hain)

Ye idioms ap ki English ko zyada natural aur fluent bana denge. Har idiom ka matlab aur ek example sentence diya gaya hai.

## Kaam Aur Progress Ke Idioms

| Idiom | Roman Urdu Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Piece of cake | Bohot aasan kaam | This task was a piece of cake for me. |
| Hit the ground running | Foran tez raftar se shuru karna | We need to hit the ground running on this project. |
| Get the ball rolling | Kaam shuru karwana | Let's get the ball rolling on this task. |
| Back to square one | Wapas shuruaat mein aa jana | Our plan failed, we're back to square one. |
| Cut corners | Kaam mein kami chhorna (jaldi mein) | Don't cut corners on quality just to save time. |
| On the same page | Ek jaisi samajh rakhna | Let's make sure we're on the same page before starting. |
| Think outside the box | Alag/creative tareeqe se sochna | We need to think outside the box to solve this. |
| Call it a day | Din ka kaam yahin khatam karna | I'm tired, let's call it a day. |
| Under the weather | Thora bemaar mehsoos karna | I'm feeling a bit under the weather today. |
| Keep me in the loop | Mujhe update karte rehna | Please keep me in the loop about this project. |

## Time Aur Deadline Ke Idioms

| Idiom | Roman Urdu Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Beat the clock | Waqt se pehle kaam khatam karna | We managed to beat the clock and finish early. |
| Down to the wire | Aakhri lamhat tak | The project went down to the wire, but we finished it. |
| In the nick of time | Aakhri waqt par | We submitted the report in the nick of time. |
| Around the clock | Chaubees ghante | The team worked around the clock to fix the bug. |
| Time flies | Waqt bohot jaldi guzarta hai | Time flies when you're busy with work. |

## Baat Cheet Ke Idioms

| Idiom | Roman Urdu Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Break the ice | Baat cheet ki shuruaat karna | He told a joke to break the ice in the meeting. |
| Get straight to the point | Seedha asal baat par aana | Let's get straight to the point, we don't have much time. |
| Long story short | Mukhtasar mein | Long story short, we finished the project on time. |
| Speak of the devil | Jiski baat ho rahi thi wo aa gaya | Speak of the devil, here he comes! |
| Let's touch base | Thori si baat cheet karna (update lena) | Let's touch base tomorrow about the project. |
| Food for thought | Sochne wali baat | That's an interesting idea, food for thought. |

## Problem Solving Ke Idioms

| Idiom | Roman Urdu Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| A blessing in disguise | Zahiri musibat jo asal mein faida ho | Losing that job was a blessing in disguise. |
| Bite the bullet | Mushkil faisla himmat se karna | We just have to bite the bullet and fix this now. |
| Learn the ropes | Naya kaam seekhna | It took me a month to learn the ropes here. |
| Get the hang of it | Kisi cheez ki adat/mahaarat aana | Don't worry, you'll get the hang of it soon. |
| See eye to eye | Kisi baat par mutafiq hona | We finally saw eye to eye on the design. |
| Iron out the details | Chhoti mushkilat sort karna | Let's iron out the details before the launch. |

## Roz Marra Ki Zindagi Ke Idioms

| Idiom | Roman Urdu Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Once in a blue moon | Kabhi kabhar (bohot kam) | I eat fast food once in a blue moon. |
| A piece of my mind | Khul kar apna gussa/khayal jataana | I gave him a piece of my mind about the delay. |
| Costs an arm and a leg | Bohot mehnga hona | That laptop costs an arm and a leg. |
| Better late than never | Der aaye durust aaye | You finally called me back — better late than never. |
| The best of both worlds | Dono cheezon ka faida | Working remotely gives me the best of both worlds. |
| Hang in there | Himmat rakho | I know it's tough right now, just hang in there. |

## Practice Tip
